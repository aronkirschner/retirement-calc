How this model works
Each year the portfolio earns a blended return based on your stock/bond mix. During working years, contributions are added (and grown with inflation). Starting at retirement, you withdraw the amount needed to cover spending (also inflation-adjusted) minus any Social Security or pension income (which starts at the age you set, and also rises with inflation).

"Today's dollars" divides nominal balances by cumulative inflation, so you can see what the money is actually worth in today's purchasing power. The "nest egg needed" is the present-value lump sum — in today's dollars — that would just barely fund all net withdrawals from retirement through your plan-to age, discounted at the real return rate. Your actual projected portfolio at retirement (also in today's dollars) should comfortably exceed this number.

Limitations: Uses constant average returns — no sequence-of-returns risk, no taxes, no healthcare shocks, no account for required minimum distributions. Real planning should add a meaningful buffer for all of these. For a US 70/30 portfolio, a withdrawal rate around 3.5–4% of the starting balance is the common rule of thumb.
